How to Use:

1 - Pyramids

The pyramid pattern consists of 3-dimensional looking pyramids. If your second number after 6 is a 1, then you have rolled a pyramid pattern. To draw a pyramid pattern, pick a side and draw a triangle. Then draw a line off of the top and down the side of the triangle, and then draw a smaller triangle on the inside of the first one you drew. The next number after this determines the variant of pyramids you will draw. These are down below:

Pyramid Patterns

2 - Temple

The temple pattern is drawing by making a section, and then making another section in the opposite direction as the one you just made, and going underneath the first one, and so on. The number after the 2 represents Whether you will start with an + or a X (1 and 2 respectively) pattern. (type 1 is an example of a + pattern, for type 2 it just determines the pattern you draw first, and then draw the second one afterwards and continue this pattern.) The next number (fourth) will determine which type of temple pattern you draw. Type 1 is either an a + or an X shape, and type 2 is a star shape starting with either a + or X and alternating between the other pattern. Two examples are below:

This will be followed by a pattern number, and a set of line patterns to follow to complete the pattern. The patterns are to be drawn in the same order that you draw the pattern.
