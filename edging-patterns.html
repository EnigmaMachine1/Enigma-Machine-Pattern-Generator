How to Use:

One thing that all edging patterns have in common is that they start from the outside edges of the section you are working in, hence the name. There are a few different ways of going about this. 3-1 and 3-2 codes both involve outlining. I suppose it might be more accurate to call it inlining. This involves outlining the edges of the shape towards the center. This will make more sense in the parts of this guide devoted to those types of edging patterns below. 3-3 and 3-4 codes on the other hand involve starting at the edges of a section, and working your way inward using some kind of repeating shape.

After that, the next numbers in this kind of code denote the amount and type of patterns that will be used to fill in the lines. The patterns here are shape patterns, so see section 2 for deciphering which patterns correspond to the pattern numbers.

In this type of pattern, we start at the beginning, and follow the same exact route that we took when drawing the first lines, and each time we skip to another one of the sections on our way to the center we switch to the next pattern in the series, and so on, repeating the patterns this way until the shape is filled. If it makes you feel a bit better about it, you could instead do the whole thing at once by drawing one of the outline lines, filling in the pattern, and then moving to the next line. It is a bit easier to keep track of your work this way.

Shape edging is similar to standard edging, but instead of using the patterns from standard edging we roll a shape and use that shape to do the edging. When doing shape edging, if your pattern number is 3, you put 3 layers on the shape. If the pattern number is 2, you would put two layers on your shape. Treat every number after 3-4 as if it has a 2 in front of it, and that will be the shape roll for the shapes you are edging with.

For example, this would be the process if you rolled 3-4-1-3-4.1-1.3-5.1, your shape number would be 1, which means a circle according to the shape codes, and your 3 after the 1 would be your pattern number, which is 3.
